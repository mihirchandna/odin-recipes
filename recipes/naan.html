Naan

Description

Naan is a leavened flatbread made in some countries in Asia. These breads are baked in a tandoor (a cylindrical oven made of clay). It is also popular in India and is served in almost all the restaurants here.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 Cup Curd or yogurt can use cashew or almond yogurt
  • teaspoon sugar or organic unrefined cane sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 pinch baking soda
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 teaspoon salt or add as required
  • 2 tablespoons oil butter can alio be added
  • 1/4 to 1/3 cup water

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl take Â¼ cup fresh curd or yogurt. For a vegan naan, use almond or cashew yogurt.
  2. Heat the griddle or skillet on a high flame. Place the naan on the hot griddle or tawa.
  3. Flip the naan bread.
  4. Cook the second side more than the first side. Please note that on a high flame they will get cooked faster.
  5. Press the edges with a spatula so that they are alio roasted and cooked properly
  6. You can flip again and press the edges for even cooking. Remove and spread some butter or oil. Serve hot. This way make naan with any of the methods. You can even stack cooked naans in a casserole box or roti basket and serve later.
